Khand is the original unrefined Indian cane sugar — crystallised from pure sugarcane juice without bleaching or chemical processing. Its pale amber colour comes not from impurity but from the natural molasses that remain. It carries a warmth white sugar cannot replicate: a faint note of vanilla, a lingering sweetness that settles rather than spikes.

ZUCERO Khand is sourced from small farms in Uttar Pradesh where the harvest tradition has been uninterrupted for generations. Each batch is slow-dried and coarse-ground by hand, ensuring the crystal structure remains intact.
  • 100% raw sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum)
  • Zero additives, preservatives, or bleaching agents
  • Contains trace iron, calcium, and potassium
  • Glycaemic index lower than refined white sugar
  • No artificial colouring or flavouring
Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Keep sealed in an airtight container once opened. Shelf life: 18 months from production date. Because Khand is unrefined, it may absorb humidity and form mild clumps — this does not affect quality. Break apart gently before use.

The Story of Khand

Before sugar became white,
there was Khand.

In the kitchens of north India, Khand was not a substitute for sugar. It was the original. The sweet choice of grandmothers, of temple offerings, of the first cup of chai poured in the morning. White sugar arrived later — refined for uniformity, for commerce, for a different kind of world.

We went back to find what was lost. What we found was not nostalgia — it was superiority. A complexity of flavour that white sugar cannot replicate. A sweetness that lingers rather than spikes. A product that earns a place in the modern kitchen not because it is old, but because it is better.

Ritual Suggestions

Ways to use Khand

Morning Chai

Replace refined sugar with Khand in your daily chai. Half a teaspoon is enough — it sweetens gently, without overpowering the spice.

Baking & Desserts

Khand adds a depth to kheer, halwa, and baked goods that refined sugar cannot. Use 1:1 as a substitute — the colour will be deeper, the flavour fuller.

Ayurvedic Tonics

Used in traditional chyawanprash and herbal preparations, Khand is considered saatvik — pure, sattvic, and digestively gentle.

As a Gift

Present Khand the way you would present fine tea or wine. It is a sweetener with a story — the kind of thing that makes someone pause before they taste.
